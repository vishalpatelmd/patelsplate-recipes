Gujarati Style Daal

A sweet and tangy lentil soup, a staple in our family.

Back to all recipes

Ingredients

    For the Daal:

  • 1 cup Toor Daal (split pigeon peas)
  • 2-3 green chiles, slit
  • 1 small piece of ginger
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • 1 small tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon jaggery or sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt, to taste

    For the Tempering (Vaghār):

  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 dry red chiles
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi dana)
  1. Wash the toor daal thoroughly and soak it for at least 30 minutes.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked daal, 4 cups of water, turmeric powder, green chiles, ginger, peanuts, and chopped tomato.
  3. Pressure cook for 4-5 whistles on medium heat. Let the pressure release naturally.
  1. Once the daal is cooked, mash it lightly with a whisk or a wooden spoon to get a smooth consistency.
  2. Add jaggery, lemon juice, and salt. Bring to a boil and simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  1. **For the Vaghār (tempering):** In a small pan, heat ghee or oil. Once hot, add mustard seeds. When they splutter, add cumin seeds, cloves, cinnamon stick, dry red chiles, asafoetida, and fenugreek seeds.
  2. Add the curry leaves and sauté for a few seconds. Be careful as the leaves may splutter.
  3. Pour the hot tempering over the simmering daal. Stir gently.
  4. Serve hot with rice and a side of vegetables.
